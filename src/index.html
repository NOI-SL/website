What is International Olympiad in Informatics ?

IOI stands for International Olympiad in Informatics and is one of the eight international Science Olympiads, held annualy.

It is an algorithmic programming competition for students from secondary education schools. The contestants should have studied in school at the beginning of the year and under 20 years (born on or after 1st of July). Half the contestents are awarded medals at IOI.

Contestents have to write programs in C++, Pascal or Java to solve 3 tasks in 5 hours each day, for two contest days.

IOI 2018 was held in Japan with the participation of 335 participants from 87 countries around the world.

IOI 2019 is ti be held in Azerbaijan, a country in the Eurasian region.
